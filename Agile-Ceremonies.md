## What are the main Agile ceremonies, and what is their purpose?
- Daily Standups:
  Daily standups are short meetings used to discuss progress and identify blockers. Mostly used to update everyone on what you're doing for the day, what you've done before, and any blockers.

- Sprint Planning (scrum) vs Continuous Prioritization (Kanban):
  Sprint planning is done at the beginning of the sprint. Used to identify what's to be done in the sprint, and how it should be done.

  Continous Prioritization on the other hand refers to the ongoing process of reevaluation, ranking, pulling the tasks with the highest priorities. Kanban teams also have planning stages, but not on a fixed recurring schedule with formal sprint planning

- Sprint Retrospective:
  Sprint retrospectives are meetings used to review what was successful during a sprint, and what can be improved. It is an important aspect of the Agile framework where rapid feedback and improvements are crucial. Teams discuss solutions and develop a plan of action for things that didn't work out. \

- Backlog Refienement:
  I believe this refers to sprint reviews. These meetings happen at the end of a sprint, where teams discuss what was accomplished. Teams would show which backlog items are done, and receive feedback for that. 


## How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?
I believe teams would prioritize written or recorded communication, with less live meetings. If live meetings were to be held, a sensible time would be chosen. Using a shared calenday system (such as Google's calendar) makes it easy to keep track of meetings that need to be attended.

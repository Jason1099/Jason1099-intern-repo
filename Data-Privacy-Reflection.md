## How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?
based on [oaic.gov.au](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-guidance-for-organisations-and-government-agencies/preventing-preparing-for-and-responding-to-data-breaches/data-breach-preparation-and-response/part-3-responding-to-data-breaches-four-key-steps#:~:text=Step%201:%20Contain%20the%20data,simultaneously%20or%20in%20quick%20succession.)

1. Contain the data breach to prevent further data compromise
2. Assess the data breach. Gather facts, evaluate risks including potential harm to any individuals, and take action to remediate any risk of harm
3. Notify individuals and the Commisioner if requried
4. Review the incident and take preemptive actions to prevent future breaches

Storing data:
storing really depends on the data format and how secure we need it to be, but in general:
1. In a cloud provided by the company, or in a local secure drive. Ensure these are secure. It is good to have a backup of any sensitive data in case they get deleted in some of our devices

Sharing data:
Based on what I've read:
1. Try to only share encypted data to avoid any stolen data

Data disposal:
Based on what I've read:
1. Do a software overwrite or wipe to make data unrecoverable
2. Physically destroy any drives if neccesary
3. If they are on physical documents, shred them or in bins designed for them

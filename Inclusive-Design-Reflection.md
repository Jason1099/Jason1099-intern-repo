## Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?
> According to the WHO, "Vulnerability is defined as ‘The conditions determined by physical, social, economic, and environmental factors or processes which increase the susceptibility of an individual, a community, assets, or systems to the impacts of hazards’. "
> According to the NIH, "A vulnerable population is a group of individuals who are at increased risk for health problems and health disparities."
Examples include: <br/>
- The very young and old
- Individuals with disabilities, illnesses, or communication barriers
- Racial and ethnical minorities,
- Homeless people

It is found that digital technologies may have exacerbated already existing inequalities. Digital spaces can and are used to discriminate and violate human rights. 

Some challenges then include: <br/>
1. greater difficulty in gaining access to digital devices, services and the internet
2. Limited digital literacy and skills to effectively use digitial technologies
3. Needs of vulnerable groups are overlooked in the development of these digital spaces

## What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? 

Some principles when working with neurodivergent teams:
1. Transparency; backlog should be made visible, and be clear on how the work should be done
2. meet daily: A brief daily meeting allows smooth progress assesment and adapt plans when needed
3. Build Bonds

Some design Principles for neurodivergent individuals:
1. Reduce visual and cognitive overload
2. Grant control over motion and sound options
3. Have customisable display options e.g., fonts, text size, colours
4. Clear language

## How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism?
According to leedsautismaim.org.uk:

In the case of autism, it is important to reduce sensory overload. 
Some ways to make content in apps and websites more suited for autism:
1. Use a pastel background color when possible
2. Customisable font and colors
3. have a drop down menu for all main pages
4. Easy to read font like Arial

In the case of ADHD, we need to consider attention span and distractions
Some ways to make content in apps and websites suited for ADHD:
1. Reduce moving elements
2. Simplify designs and limit the number of items on a page
3. Limit clickable buttons
4. Have leading instructions to make it easier to get what they need, e.g., "read this first"

## How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting?
Some ways are included in point number 2. Other than that:
1. Flexible work environements, i.e. where they work, and what they use when working
2. Clear, written communication: written instructions can be helpful in reducing anxiety and ambiguity

# Research
## What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?
1. Phishing: Employees getting targeted by deceptive emails/messages
2. Insecure Networks: Hackers breach data through the wifi network
3. Persoanal Device Insecurity: Personal devices with weak antivirus software, weak firewalls, or getting the device stolen due to it being left alone without being locked.

## What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?
1. Use MFA (Multi-Factor Authorisation) and strong passwords
2. Keep software updated
3. Be cautious of what we download

## Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?
this is important to prevent anyone from using our computers without consent, where they will be able to steal data, and misuse our accounts. 

## How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?
I should report and block the sender's account, and share it with others to let them know. Make sure to never open these links without inspecting the urls.

## What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?
A strong password should be relatively long (around 12-16 characters) and consists of a mix letters, symbols, and numbers to ensure the password cannot be guessed. It should not be made based on obvious patterns <br/>
A password manager is a good way of storing passwords for all our different accounts, generating strong passwords. It is also convenient as it can autofill login credentials. 

## Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?
2FA is important because it adds a second layer of security to the account. If our password was somehow stolen, attackers wouldn't be able to use our account without that second security level, such as SMS or Biometrics. <br/>
It should be enabled on important accounts with important data, such as personal/work email accounts, financial accounts, social media, etc.

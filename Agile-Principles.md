# Research
## What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?
Agile management is where it focuses on flexibility and team collaboration. It is flexible in a sense that it is easy to adjust things throughout the project. Agile projects are comprised of multiple short planning and sprint sessions, allowing for feedback and changes in between sprints.

## What are the core values and principles of Agile?
 Based on the [Agile Manifesto](https://agilemanifesto.org/iso/en/principles.html):
 1. Satisfy customers through continous and early deliverables of software
 2. Welcome changes in requirements
 3. Deliver working software frequently
 4. Business people and developers should work together
 5. Projects should be built around motivated people, and provide support to them
 6. Face to face conversation is the most effective and efficient method of communication
 7. Working software is the primary measure of progress
 8. Agile development promotes sustainable development.
 9. Attention to technicality and good design will enhance agility
 10. Keep things simple
 11. The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams
 12. Have regular sessions to reflect on how to become more efficient.

## What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?
Kanban is more of a visual management method, where we can easily see the list of tasks in their respective phases / columns. <br/>
Scrum is specifically designed for planning iterative development, by planning different sprints. This also makes it easier to adapt to changes. <br/>

I think it's not a matter of when each should be used, because we can just use both at the same time and get both benefits. But primarily:
1. Kanban can be used and incorporated into any projects, and improves work visibility, productivity and fosters continous improvement
2. Scrum should be used when we know that the area we are working on will experience frequent changes.

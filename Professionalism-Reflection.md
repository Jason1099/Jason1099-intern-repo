# Research
## What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment
Communication wise:
1. Respectully wait for others to finish talking before talking yourself, vs cutting others
2. Clear, concise language, and be polite, vs being unfiltered and rude.

Tardiness:
1. Being punctual and meeting deadlines, vs always being late and disregarding deadlines

Conduct and Appearance:
1. Wear clean and neat clothing, without being too formal, vs using innapropriate clothing
2. Be respectful and polite in office situations, such as keeping noise levels down, and keeping cleanliness, vs being inconsiderate and disrupting everyone else's work.

## What does respectful communication look like in remote teams?
I think the principles that apply in a normal, physical team can apply here, with some added points for that digital aspect
1. Listen to others, and avoid interupting. Mute our mics when we don't intend to speak.
2. Choosing the correct channel to send a message. This will depend on the context and importance of the message, along with the preferences of the receiver.
3. Be mindful of the tone we are using, especially if we ever encounter a disagreement
4. Try to come prepared in a meeting, such as testing our internet connections, microphones, and be in time.
5. Be mindful when we screenshare anything.
6. Respond to feedback with confirmation, and perhaps a good, polite discussion
7. In general, just be polite and friendly

## How to receive and give feedback
When receiving feedback, it is important to:
1. Listen (or read) to what is being said carefully and intently.
2. Ask for clarification if anything is confusing
3. Be open to suggestions and advice
4. follow it up by implementing said feedback, or having a follow discussion

When giving feedback:
1. Try to focus more on the behavior, instead of the person. Doing so makes it less offensive or accusing
2. Be balanced; try to talk about what can be improved/weaknesses, along with what went well/strengths
3. Be realistic; as in give feedback on things that can be changed, and is in control of the person.
4. Be specific; Don't be too general or broad when giving feedback, and try to give examples that they can look to.

## Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?
I experienced great teamwork a couple of times during university, and alot of them before that, although wouldn't really call it proffesional. What made proffesional teams have great teamwork from what I experienced, was that everyone created a good "vibe" when working together. This was done when everyone was respectfull yet friendly, was clear in communicating what they were doing or had trouble with, provided good suggestions and feedback, were reliable and accountable. All of this made the team work very well together, while keeping it professional

## What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?
I think a simple answer to this is to try word things out in my head before talking it out. Another way is to try make points of what I'm trying to say and review that before hand. It will also come down to practice and habits. 

## How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?
I would simply be friendly, try to start small talk when it feels right. This would include thinking about my body language, tone and expressions. In general, just try to have fun and enjoy working, whilst keeping things proffesional

## How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?
It would depend on the context of the meeting, such as the agenda. In general:
1. Make sure all equipment is working, and am in an environment appropriate for the meeting
2. Make quick points of what I would like to ask or say
3. Read any required material, or just read about the topic in the agenda beforehand
4. Be ready to take notes

## How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?
I would start with contacting them through email when not too urgent, and discord if I need a quicker response. If I don't get a response within a reasonable time range, I would try re-message them with the same channel. If I don't get a response even after that, I would try other forms of contact (SMS or Private messages), or contact another colleague or supervisor as well.
